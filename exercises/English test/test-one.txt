## PART 1: READING COMPREHENSION (40 points)

### Read the following text and answer the questions.

**The Future of Remote Work**

The COVID-19 pandemic fundamentally changed the way we work. While many companies initially viewed remote work as a temporary solution, research now suggests that hybrid and remote working arrangements are here to stay. A recent survey by the McKinsey Global Institute found that 35% of workers would prefer to work from home at least three days a week if given the choice.

Companies are beginning to recognize both the advantages and challenges of remote work. On the positive side, employees report higher levels of job satisfaction and better work-life balance. Productivity has not decreased as many feared; in fact, some studies indicate it has improved. Cost savings are significant too—companies save money on office space and utilities.

However, remote work does present challenges. Managers struggle with maintaining team cohesion and company culture when employees are scattered across different locations. The lack of face-to-face interaction can make it harder for young professionals to develop essential skills and build meaningful relationships with colleagues. Additionally, communication can become more complicated when team members are in different time zones.

The most promising approach appears to be hybrid working, which combines the best of both worlds. Employees come to the office on specific days for collaboration and team-building, but have flexibility to work from home for focused individual tasks. Tech companies like Shopify and Dropbox have already implemented this model with positive results.

Looking forward, the future of work will likely depend on the industry. Fields requiring hands-on work or immediate collaboration will maintain more traditional office environments, while knowledge-based industries will embrace more flexible arrangements. The key is finding the right balance for each organization's unique needs.
